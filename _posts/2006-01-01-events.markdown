<p>This schedule is current as of 9/18/2024. Expect updates before the beginning of the quarter.</p>

In-person workshops will typically be held in <b><a href="http://campusmap.ucdavis.edu/?b=115">The Physical and Data Sciences Building, previously known as PSEL (Physical Sciences & Engineering Library),</a> Seminar Room (room 1025)</b>. We anticipate making videos of instructional workshops available on our <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@maptimedavis95616">YouTube Channel</a> after the end of the quarter for those who are unable to attend in person.</p>

<h3>Lightning Talks</h3>
<p><b>Date:</b> November 1, 2024 10:00-12:00</p>
<p><b>Location:</b>The Physical and Data Sciences Building, previously known asPSEL (Physical Sciences & Engineering Library) Seminar Room (room 1025)</p>
<p><b>Description:</b> A lightning talk is a short presentation meant to spark discussion and collaboration across UC Davis geospatial community. Each speaker will have maximum 5 minutes to share what they’ve been working on. Does it need to be a finished project? NO! Works in progress are encouraged! You can think of this as research show-and-tell; a time to share your progress, get new ideas, or learn more about research happening on campus! Who can present? Anyone who is working on (or thinking about) a project involving spatial data – undergraduates, graduate students, postdocs, staff, faculty, and community members are all welcome to share. Present Your Research:  Please use this form to sign up to present by Friday, Oct. 25. Presentations are due by Oct. 30th. If you have any questions/comments/concerns, please reach out to Lauren Mabe at lmabe@ucdavis.edu.</p>
<p><b>Speaker:</b> The Community!</p>
<p><b>Prerequisites:</b> None.</p>

<h3>Contributing to Open Street Map</h3>
<p><b>Date:</b> November 5, 2024 10:00-12:00</p>
<p><b>Location:</b>The Physical and Data Sciences Building, previously known asPSEL (Physical Sciences & Engineering Library) Seminar Room (room 1025)</p>
<p><b>Description:</b> Open Street Map is a free and an open, user-contributed global dataset of infrastructure. It is similar to Google Maps except anyone can contribute to, interact with, and download the data! Learn more about this incredible resource and get started as a contributor in this beginner-friendly workshop. </p> 

<p><b>Workshop objectives:</b></p>
<p>Open and interact with OSM.</p>
<p>Create an editor account and add a feature.</p>
<p>Learn about HOTOSM (Humanitarian mapping) and how to find and edit tasks.</p>
<p>Learn how to do a query of the OSM data in QGIS and save the results to a geopackage or shapefile.</p>

<p><b>Concepts Covered:</b> Creating Spatial Data, Open Data, User-Contributed Data, Data Querying</p>
<p><b>Speaker:</b> Naomi Kalman</p>
<p>Naomi Kalman the GIS Specialist for UC Davis Campus Planning and Environmental Stewardship, supporting the operations, maintenance and administration of the university that is her alma mater.</p>
<p><b>Prerequisites:</b> None.</p>
<p><a href="https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfgvyKPWkjxZXebydqZwD_v_XXOrVC3ERcCnIPex3skY5kJpg/viewform?usp=sf_link">RSVP</a></p>
